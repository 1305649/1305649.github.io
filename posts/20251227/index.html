The spiritual and the material form a fundamental opposition within our understanding of the world. In the recent history of the West, the focus has been on creating a mainly material ontology, one which excludes underlying principles as an explanation for worldly phenomena. Following the enlightenment, this material-causal empiricism has lead to tremendous technological progress, yet the West has undeniably suffered from rejecting the importance of the spiritual elements to culture. This materialization is exactly the death of metaphysics, a field previously concerned with understanding how higher principles embody in the material. While possibly foreign to modern people, metaphysics was exactly the object of religion, and the background for knowledge in traditional thought. Through this blog, I want to explore religious texts, the decline of Western spirituality, and how similar trends have impacted culture, domestic politics, geopolitics, and our general phenomenology.

We seem to be at an inflection point of cultural decay. In fact, we seem to be at the point of cultural death as predicted by The Unread Writers. It seems that these trends were also widely recognized in the 20th century (Jung, Guenon, Spengler, Evola), and these very thoughts now ground the arguments of right-wing reactionaries in the American political landscape.
